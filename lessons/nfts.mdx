## Owning NFTs on Solana

In traditional finance, a deed proves you own a house, a stock certificate proves you own shares of a company, and a numbered art print proves you own a specific copy of a work. Each is a verifiable record of ownership, often issued by a trusted authority. **<Term id="nft">NFTs</Term> (Non-Fungible Tokens)** are the digital equivalent on a blockchain: unique tokens that prove ownership of a specific item, recorded publicly and transparently. This lesson explains what NFTs are on Solana, why they matter, how to receive and manage them with **Jupiter Wallet**, and how to stay safe.

### What Makes an NFT Different

A regular token like <Term id="sol">SOL</Term> or USDC is **fungible**, meaning every unit is identical and interchangeable. One USDC is exactly equal to any other USDC. Fungible tokens are also **divisible**; you can split 1 SOL into 0.1 SOL and 0.9 SOL, and the two parts together are still worth exactly 1 SOL.

An **NFT is non-fungible**: each one is unique, and it cannot be split. You can't send "10% of an NFT" - you either own it or you don't. Two NFTs from the same collection share style and origin, but each has its own ID, ownership history, and metadata.

What you actually own when you "own an NFT" is a record on the blockchain that points to the asset (an image, video, or file hosted on decentralized storage). That record proves you, and only you, control it from your wallet.

### Real Use Cases

NFTs are more than just profile pictures. The most common categories:

* **Profile pictures (PFPs) and art.** Collections like Mad Lads, Claynosaurz, and Solana Monkey Business often give their holders community access and <Term id="airdrop">airdrops</Term>.
* **Gaming.** NFTs can represent in-game items, characters, and land plots that you truly own and can trade outside the game itself.
* **Tickets and access.** NFTs can serve as event tickets, membership passes, or keys to gated content.
* **Domain names.** Solana Name Service domains (yourname.sol) are NFTs that you own, replacing your public address with a human-readable name.
* **Position NFTs.** A growing <Term id="defi">DeFi</Term> pattern where your position in a protocol is itself an NFT. [Jupiter Borrow](/lessons/jupiter-borrow) (and [Multiply](/lessons/jupiter-multiply)) Position NFTs store all the relevant data for the position. Because the NFT is transferable, moving it to another wallet transfers the entire position with it.

> **Critical: Never burn your Position NFT**
>
> If you use Jupiter Lend, the Position NFT is required to manage and withdraw your funds. <Term id="burn">Burning</Term> it destroys your access to the underlying assets.

### How Jupiter Wallet Handles NFTs

**Jupiter Wallet** (mobile and browser extension) is a non-custodial Solana wallet built around trading. Only you control your keys.

![Jupiter Wallet stores your NFTs](https://static.academy.jup.ag/images/illustrations/nft_wallet.avif)

**What it does:**

* Receives any NFT to your Solana address (same address as for tokens).
* **Displays your NFT collection visually.** Open the **NFTs** tab in Jupiter Mobile to browse your holdings as a grid with images and names.
* Holds your NFTs securely for easy viewing.
* Links you out to marketplaces like Magic Eden and Tensor, where buying, selling, and listing actually take place.
* Shows every transaction for review before you sign.

It's important to note that Jupiter Wallet does not run its own NFT marketplace. Buying, selling, and listing happen on third-party venues like Magic Eden or Tensor.

### Risks and How to Stay Safe

> **Careful: NFT scams are pervasive and creative**

Most lost NFTs are not stolen by hackers. They are signed away by holders who didn't read the transaction. Read every signature request carefully — if you do not recognise the action, reject it. **Your private key is never needed for legitimate operations. Ever.**

* **Drainer Signatures** - A malicious site asks you to sign what looks like a standard connection, but the transaction actually transfers your NFTs out of your wallet. Read every transaction carefully before signing and reject anything unexpected.
* **Fake Mint Pages** - Scammers clone the website of a real upcoming collection. You attempt to <Term id="minting">mint</Term> but instead lose SOL or other assets. Bookmark official sites and verify the URL twice before connecting your wallet.
* **Airdrop Bait** - A random NFT appears in your wallet with a link inside promising free SOL or rewards. The link drains your wallet. Ignore unsolicited NFTs and never click links embedded inside them.
* **DM Impersonation** - Fake support agents on Discord or X request your <Term id="seed-phrase">seed phrase</Term> or a "verification" signature under the guise of helping you. Real support will never DM you first or ask for your seed phrase.

Below is a simple mitigation checklist to consider before each transaction:

* **Read every transaction before signing.** If a popup mentions transferring NFTs you didn't intend to move, reject it.
* **Use the wallet's transaction scanner.** Jupiter Wallet flags suspicious transactions. Take warnings seriously.
* **Never type your seed phrase.** Not on a website, not in a DM, not in a "support" form. These might be [phishing attempts](/lessons/safety-phishing). Real support never asks for it.
* **Ignore unsolicited NFTs.** If a random NFT appears, don't click any link inside it.
* **Bookmark official marketplaces.** Type magiceden.io or tensor.trade directly, never click ads or DM links.
* **Use a hardware wallet** for valuable holdings. Jupiter Wallet supports Bluetooth-only Ledger, whereas the [Jupiter Extension](/lessons/jupiter-browser-extension) supports Ledger, Keystone, and Trezor.

### Tutorial: Receiving and Verifying Your First NFT

Whether someone is sending you an NFT or you just minted one, the flow is the same.

1. Open **Jupiter Wallet** (mobile or extension) and unlock it.
2. Tap **Receive** and copy your Solana address (or share the QR code).
3. Send the address to whoever is transferring the NFT, or paste it into the mint site. The transfer executes <Term id="onchain">onchain</Term> in seconds.
4. To verify it, open the **NFTs** tab inside Jupiter Mobile, the new NFT will appear in your gallery with its image and name. You can also paste your address into **solscan.io** or connect to **magiceden.io** / **tensor.trade** for more details.
5. Bookmark the marketplaces you use. Never connect through links sent by strangers.

In short: NFTs let you hold verifiable ownership directly in your wallet — receive them to your Solana address, always check what you're signing, and trade only on bookmarked marketplaces like Magic Eden or Tensor.
